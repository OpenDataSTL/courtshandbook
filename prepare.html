How to Prepare for Court

What to bring:

Please refer to your ticket (or call the court ahead of time) to determine if you should bring any specific documentation. You will want to have with you:

  • Current driver's license, and license from the time of the ticketed incident
  • Current proof of insurance, and proof of insurance during the time of the ticketed incident
  • Sufficient cash or checkbook to pay courts and citations fees
  • Any documentation related to your income, if you wish to request community service, a payment plan, or extension.
What to wear:

Some courts have a mandated dress code, and each court's dress code may vary. Please check your specific court's page to confirm the dress code. Generally speaking, use good judgment and dress more conservatively. For example, some courts do no allow shorts, sandals, bare shoulders, or hats.

What to expect:

Some courts see cases in a specific order. For example, it may be first come, first served, or they may see defendants in alphabetical order. You may be able to check on the order on the court's page or by calling ahead of time. In any case, it is critical to arrive on time or early; many times, lines form before the court's published opening time. You may want to arrange your job, transportation, and child care to accomodate several hours at the court, especially if you wish to speak with a judge (e.g. to plead not guilty, request community service) or arrange an alternative payment plan.

What if I cannot attend court?

You have several options:

  • Call the court clerk ahead of time to see if it is possible to reschedule, or to visit the court at an earlier date to pay your fine.
  • Hire a defense attorney to attend on your behalf. Sometimes, you can qualify for a volunteer attorney to help you pro bono (i.e. at no charge), or the cost is much less than you'd expect.
  • Some paper citations outline options for how to pay ahead of time, mail in proof, etc. For clarification, call the courts clerk in the municipality where you received your ticket.
Outstanding questions

Remember that the court is there to assist. Please contact your individual court for specific information related to your case.
